## 🌍 What Is ANIVERSITY?

ANIVERSITY is a living experiment in curiosity.  
It has no classrooms and no tuition.  
Here, **questions are the teachers** — each one looks impossible until imagination finds the hidden path through it.

Built on GitHub so anyone can join, ANIVERSITY connects the spirit of the **Three Bibles**:

- **Antti Bible** – ethics, empathy, and peace  
- **FPGA Bible** – logic, electronics, and creation  
- **One Hour Bible** – collaboration between human and AI  

ANIVERSITY is where all those ideas become practice.

---

## 🧩 Guiding Principles

- **Open Creation** – everything is public, forkable, and shared.  
- **Equality of Minds** – everyone is a learner, no prerequisites required.  
- **Integration** – philosophy, engineering, and art coexist.  
- **Learning by Invention** – progress by making, not memorizing.  
- **Perpetual Beta** – every answer spawns a new question.

---

## 🚀 How It Works

1. Browse the repository’s **/questions** folder.  
2. Pick any question that inspires you.  
3. Research, build, or reason until you can propose an answer.  
4. Submit your response as a **pull request** or discussion thread.  
5. When your idea changes how others think, you’ve *graduated.*

Graduation here is symbolic — recognition by peers that you turned the impossible into a proof of possibility.

---

## 🧠 Why "Impossible" Questions?

Because impossibility is perspective.  
Each challenge is a tool for exploring both logic and imagination.  
Solving one problem teaches more than memorizing a thousand facts.

---

## 💡 How to Contribute

- Fork this repo and explore `questions/`.  
- Add comments, experiments, or results in `answers/`.  
- Create new questions with markdown files:  
  `YYYY-MM-DD_your-question-title.md`  
- Share findings through issues or pull requests.

All materials are released under **MIT** — freely shareable.
